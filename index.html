Dangers of Heat

Heat waves can be dangerous, especially for vulnerable populations like the elderly, young children, and those with chronic illnesses. Prolonged exposure to high temperatures can lead to heat exhaustion, heat stroke, and other serious health issues.

It's a hot day today.

What to do during this weather:

  1. Stay hydrated by drinking plenty of water throughout the day.
  2. Wear lightweight and loose-fitting clothing to help your body stay cool.
  3. Avoid direct sunlight during peak hours (10 a.m. to 4 p.m.) and seek shade whenever possible.
  4. Use sunscreen with a high SPF to protect your skin from harmful UV rays.
  5. Limit outdoor activities and take breaks in air-conditioned or shaded areas to prevent heat exhaustion.
